The History of Pillows

Years Material Population
7000 BCE Stone While not the best start to the current comfortable designs, these pillows were actually used to avoid insects crawling into individuals’ mouths, ears and noses. Only wealthy Mesopotamians could afford these stone pillows, so they were a sign of great wealth.
3100 BCE Wood Used in ancient Egypt, especially by Pharohs
Ancient Europe Reeds, Feathers, and Straw Only upper-class people typically owned these softer pillows; however, all classes of people were allowed to use some type of pillow while sleeping, lying down or sitting in order to give them support.
580 CE Porcelain They were used to cushion patients’ wrists when Chinese doctors checked their pulses before giving diagnoses, and to support the arms of those writing with brushes to help give them a steadier hand. Additionally, travelers started carrying small porcelain pillows as sleeping aids.
1760 - 1800's Cotton, Straw, and Feathers It wasn’t until the Industrial Revolution that pillows began to be mass produced. This allowed them to be affordable enough to be used in every household.
